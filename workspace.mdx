A workspace represents a company, team, or client environment in Ugen. It is the container for chats, projects, builders, plan limits, credits, and shared settings.

## After login

After you sign in, Ugen opens the app workspace layout. The left sidebar shows your active workspace at the top, navigation links, recent activity, and your user avatar.

The workspace switcher lets you:

- See the current workspace name and plan, such as **NexaERP** on a paid plan
- Switch between available workspaces
- Identify locked workspaces that you cannot open
- Create a new workspace
- Keep chats, projects, and templates separated by company or team

## Workspace basics

- **Company name** is collected during account creation.
- **Builders** are people who can create and edit projects.
- **Projects** are generated apps, websites, tools, or internal systems.
- **Credits** are monthly usage capacity attached to the workspace plan.
- **Connectors** can be used to connect third-party tools across projects.
- **Chats** store the AI conversation that generated or modified a project.

## Project list

The projects page is the main workspace entry point for generated apps. It includes search, selection mode, grid/list views, a create-project card, and cards for existing generated projects.

Each project card shows the generated product name, preview thumbnail, last edited state, and project actions. Use this page when you want to reopen a generated app without searching through chat history.

## Chats

Chats are important in Ugen because every generated app starts from an AI conversation. Open **Chats** to return to previous prompts, continue a project, or inspect the project connected to a chat.

When a chat has a generated project, opening it brings you back to the project builder where you can continue prompting, open Settings, inspect the generated dashboard, publish the app, or switch into Code.

## Sidebar navigation

- **Home** opens the prompt builder.
- **Projects** lists generated projects.
- **Chats** lists previous AI conversations.
- **Templates** opens reusable project starters.
- **Recents** gives quick access to recently used work.

## Recommended workspace setup

1. Create the account with the correct company name.
2. Choose the plan that matches your expected number of builders and projects.
3. Keep experiments separate from production projects.
4. Use clear project names so generated apps are easy to find.
5. Review credits and limits before production launches.

## Pro workspace notes

On a Pro workspace, teams usually have more room for real client work: more projects, more builders, more monthly credits, and higher production limits. Use separate workspaces when different clients should not see each other's chats, apps, files, or users.
