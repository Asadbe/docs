Your Ugen account is the identity you use to access company workspaces, create projects, and manage generated apps.

Open account settings from the profile button in the bottom-left sidebar.

## Login methods

Ugen supports:

- Username and password login
- Google sign in
- Google sign up

## Account fields

During registration, Ugen asks for:

- Company name
- User login
- Email
- Password

## Account and workspace access

Your account can belong to one or more workspaces. The workspace switcher controls which company or client environment you are currently using. Always check the active workspace before creating a new project, inviting users, or publishing an app.

If a teammate cannot see a project, first confirm that they are signed in with the correct account and have access to the correct workspace.

## Profile settings

  <img src="/images/ugen-account-profile-settings-redacted.png" alt="Ugen profile settings modal" />
</Frame>

The **Profile** tab controls personal account details:

- **Full Name** is the display name shown across the workspace.
- **Email Address** is used for account identity and communication.
- **Phone Number** can be used for phone-based workflows when configured.
- **Login / Username** is the login identifier for password sign-in.
- **Account Type** shows the account category, such as Member.
- **Assigned Role** shows the current workspace role.
- **Login Sessions** lists active devices and browsers, with an option to remove sessions.

## Billing

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/ugen-account-billing-redacted.png" alt="Ugen account billing modal with balance, invoice overview, and transactions" />
</Frame>

The **Billing** tab shows billing and workspace payment information:

- **Current balance** shows funds available for project usage and upcoming charges.
- **Top up balance** lets the owner add funds.
- **Invoice overview** shows the active plan, total amount, and expiration date.
- **Transactions** lists billing activity by project, amount, type, date, and status.

Use Billing to confirm the workspace plan before building or publishing production projects.

## Best practices

- Use a work email for team projects.
- Keep your password private and rotate it if it is exposed.
- Use the same login method each time to avoid creating duplicate accounts.
- Confirm you are in the correct company workspace before building production projects.
